Storytelling

EasyDonate is a non-profit Chinese platform that aims at sharing donation information to people who are willing to donate their extra or old items to people living in a poor area.

It’s a project for Peking University Web Product Hackathon held in the summer of 2013. This hackathon attracted talented product managers, designers, and developers all over the world.

Interviews

We interviewed 43 participants with detailed questions about their unused items at home. The interviews aimed more directly at understanding the category, quantity, and condition of those items.

  • All interviewees had donation experience and agreed that donation is one of the best ways to deal with idle items.
  • Most of the interviewees found it difficult to donate unless some nonprofit organizations come to collect. The lack of ways of donation is one of their biggest concerns.
  • Most interviewees are willing to cover shipment fee to donate.
  • Some interviewees valued the reputation of the NGOs and cared very much about whether the NGOs successfully delivered the donation or not.

Persona

We created two personas based on the data we gathered from the interviews. These two personas helped us understand our target users’ realities, needs, and expectations.

Conceptual Model

This conceptual model demonstrates the donation relationship between NGOs and people. The information asymmetry draws attention to our top of mind concerns. Our team tried to use EasyDonate platform to balance such a gap for those who seek ways to make donations.

Wireframes

Over the following three weeks, our team entered into a very rapid cycle of prototyping and iteration with the concept. We asked over a dozen interviewees to interpret our ideas and prototypes. This process gave us the chance to discover problems early on and continuously adapt.
